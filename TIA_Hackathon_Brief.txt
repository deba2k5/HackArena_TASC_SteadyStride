1. Background & Context 
In services businesses, billing a client is surprisingly manual. Each month, timesheets arrive 
from many sources and in many formats — clean Excel sheets, scanned PDFs, and even 
photos of handwritten attendance registers. Finance teams manually clean and consolidate this 
data, feed it into an ERP (e.g., SAP) to run payroll, generate invoices, check each invoice 
against business rules, sort them, and finally email them to clients. Every step is slow, error-
prone, and hard to track. 
Your challenge is to design and prototype a Touchless Invoice Agent (TIA): an AI-driven system 
that takes timesheet inputs from any channel and any format and carries them all the way to 
validated, dispatched invoices — with humans stepping in only when needed. “Touchless” is the 
goal: the happy path should require zero manual intervention, while still keeping a human in the 
loop for exceptions. 
This is a realistic, end-to-end agentic-AI problem. It rewards teams that combine document AI 
(OCR / handwriting recognition), workflow orchestration, sensible UX, and trustworthy AI 
practices (accuracy indicators and human oversight). 
2. The Problem Statement 
Build a Touchless Invoice Agent that provides a touchless experience for users to 
dispatch invoices starting from timesheet input. 
The agent should ingest timesheets from multiple channels, convert mixed-format data 
(including handwritten documents) into a single ERP-uploadable format, orchestrate invoice 
generation, validate the invoices against business rules, dispatch them according to client-
specific rules, and give every stakeholder transparency through dashboards and an AI 
assistant. Three personas interact with the system:

4. What to Build (Functional Challenges) 
Tackle as many of the following modules as your time allows. Depth on a few well-executed 
modules beats shallow coverage of all. 
4.1 Client Configuration & Master Data 
• A setup screen to onboard a client and configure its input channels and invoice dispatch 
rules. 
• Per-client parameters that drive downstream processing (currency, billing entity, 
validation profile). 
4.2 Multi-Channel Timesheet Ingestion 
• Read timesheets from at least two channels (e.g., a mailbox/email and a portal upload; 
bonus for an online timesheet app). 
• Accept both structured Excel files and unstructured PDFs/images, including handwritten 
data. 
4.3 AI Extraction & ERP-Ready Conversion (core challenge) 
• Use OCR / Intelligent Document Processing to extract data from PDFs and handwritten 
images. 
• Normalize and consolidate everything into a single ERP-uploadable (e.g., SAP-style) 
Excel format. 
• Show an AI accuracy/confidence score per document and allow human review before 
release. 
4.4 Invoice Generation Orchestration 
• Simulate or integrate a bot/ERP step that takes the consolidated file, “processes payroll,” 
and generates invoices. 
• A stub/mock ERP is acceptable — the focus is the orchestration and data flow, not a real 
SAP instance. 
4.5 Invoice Validation 
• Validate each generated invoice against a configurable rule set (define your own “BTP-
style” parameters). 
• Flag failures and route them to a human-in-the-loop review. 
4.6 Dispatch Rules & Tracking 
• A configuration screen to define per-client dispatch rules (e.g., order bills ascending by 
spend amount or salary). 
• Dispatch invoices per the rule and track progress/status on a dashboard. 
4.7 Client Portal 
• Let clients submit timesheets, review and approve invoices, and raise queries for FinOps 
to answer. 
4.8 Cross-Cutting Capabilities (judged across all modules) 
• AI accuracy indicators shown across timesheets, invoices, and dispatch.

6. Evaluation Criteria 
Submissions will be judged on the following weighted criteria: 
Criterion Weight What judges look for 
End-to-end “touchless” flow 25% A working path from timesheet input to dispatched 
invoice. 
AI / document intelligence 20% Quality of extraction, especially handwritten/PDF, with 
accuracy indicators. 
Trust & human-in-the-loop 15% Sensible review checkpoints; graceful handling of low-
confidence cases. 
UX & dashboards 15% Clarity of portal, config screens, dashboard, and AI chat. 
Technical execution 15% Architecture, code quality, robustness of the demo. 
Innovation & presentation 10% Originality and a clear, compelling demo/pitch.

8. Suggested Tools & Tech (optional) 
Use anything you like. These are starting points, not requirements: 
• Frontend: React / Next.js, or any framework you are comfortable with. 
• Backend: Node.js/Express, Python/FastAPI, or similar. 
• Document AI / OCR: open-source OCR (e.g., Tesseract), cloud document-AI services, 
or vision-capable LLMs for handwriting. 
• AI assistant: any LLM API for the context-aware chat and extraction reasoning. 
• Data/ERP: mock ERP with a simple service or spreadsheet; a real ERP is not required. 
9. Tips to Win 
• Nail one complete vertical slice first (e.g., Excel → invoice → dispatch), then add 
channels and handwriting. 
• Treat the handwritten-document extraction as the hardest and most differentiating part 
— show your confidence handling. 
• Make demo tell a story across the three personas; judges remember a clear narrative. 
• Don’t hide the AI — surface accuracy scores and human-in-the-loop moments; trust is 
part of the score. 
10. FAQ 
Question Answer 
Do we need a real SAP system? No. Mock or stub the ERP. We care about the flow, data handling, 
and AI. 
What is “BTP” in validation? Treat it as a placeholder for a configurable business validation 
parameter set — define your own. 
Must we build all modules? No. Depth and a working end-to-end slice beat broad but shallow 
coverage. 
Can we use commercial AI 
APIs? 
Yes, if you can run the demo. Have a fallback in case of 
connectivity limits.
